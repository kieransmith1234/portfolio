Some of my uni modules

Cognitive Neural Networks

In my cognitive neural networks module, I studied neural network architecture, learning algorithms, and pattern recognition. Topics included feedforward and recurrent neural networks, backpropagation, deep learning, convolutional neural networks, and generative adversarial networks. It was a comprehensive exploration of AI and its practical applications.

Natural Computation

In my natural computation module during my computer science degree, I explored various computational models inspired by nature. Topics included genetic algorithms, evolutionary computation, swarm intelligence, and artificial immune systems. I gained insights into how these techniques can solve complex optimization and search problems, providing alternative approaches to traditional algorithms.

Concurrent Programming

In my Concurrent Programming module, I focused on designing and developing programs that can execute multiple tasks simultaneously. Topics covered included concurrency models, synchronization mechanisms such as locks and semaphores, parallel computing, and distributed systems. I learned how to handle challenges like race conditions and deadlocks while improving program performance and efficiency.

Database Design

In my Database Design module, I studied conceptual, logical, and physical database design, normalization, entity-relationship modeling, and SQL. The focus was on data modeling, query optimization, and database management systems, emphasizing data integrity, security, and scalability for effective data storage and retrieval.

Theory of Computing

In my Theory of Computing module, I explored fundamental concepts in computational theory. Topics covered included automata theory, formal languages, Turing machines, computational complexity, and the theory of NP-completeness. I gained insights into the limits of computation, algorithm analysis, and the classification of computational problems.
